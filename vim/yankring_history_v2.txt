Given\begin{equation}  f(x) = e^{\sin x}\end{equation}evaluate $\ddx{f}{x}$ on the interval $\[0,2\pi\]$ using $N$ degrees of freedom. \subsection{Centered finite differences}We approximate the dervative using a first-order centered finite difference\begin{equation}  \ddx{f}{x} \approx \frac{f_{i+1} - f_{i-1}}{2h}\end{equation}In order to enforce periodicity, we assume $f(N+1) = f(1)$.\subsection{Galerkin finite element with linear bases}The Galerkin finite element matrix equation takes the form\begin{equation}\begin{bmatrix} \langle \phi_i \phi_j \rangle \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} g_j \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \sum_k f_k \langle \ddx{\phi_k}{x} \phi_i \rangle \end{bmatrix}\end{equation}where the inner product of $\phi_i\phi_j$ is a tri-diagonal matrix for linear elements. The diagonal is given by integrating $\phi_i\phi_i$ over the interval $\[0,1\]$\begin{equation}  \int_0^1 x^2 \dx{x} = \frac{x^3}{3}\Bigg|_0^1 = \frac{1}{3}\end{equation}The off-diagonal terms are given by\begin{equation}  \int_0^1 x(1-x) \dx{x} = \frac{x^2}{2} - \frac{x^3}{3}\Bigg|_0^1 = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{6}\end{equation}\pagebreakThe RHS is a matrix-vector multiplication where the matrix is tridiagonal, with upper diagonal elements given by the inner product of $\ddx{\phi_i}{x} \phi_j$\begin{align}  \int_0^1 \phantom{-}1 \cdot x \dx{x} &= \frac{x^2}{2}\Bigg|_0^1 = \phantom{-}\half  \intertext{and the lower diagonal is given by}  \int_0^1 -1 \cdot x \dx{x} &= \frac{x^2}{2}\Bigg|_0^1 = -\half\end{align}giving the whole matrix equation\begin{equation}  \def\arraystretch{1.5}h \begin{bmatrix}   \frac{1}{3} & \frac{1}{6} &             &             &             & \frac{1}{6} \\  \frac{1}{6} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{1}{6} &             &             & \\              &             & \ddots      & \ddots      & \ddots      & \\  \frac{1}{6} &             &             & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{1}{3} & \frac{1}{6}\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle{\ddx{f}{x}_1} \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle{\ddx{f}{x}_N} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}   \phantom{-}0 & \half & & & -\half \\  -\half & 0 & \half \\         & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots \\  \phantom{-}\half  & & -\half & 0 & \half\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} f_1 \\ \vdots \\ f_N \end{bmatrix}\end{equation}where the LHS must be weighted by the element size $h$. To enforce periodicity, the corner elements of the matrices must be included. The off-diagonal matrix elements not explicitly specified are zero.,V
